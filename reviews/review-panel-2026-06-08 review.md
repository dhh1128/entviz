## Executive summary

The entviz repo is in solid operational shape (committed rulesets, gated release/deploy workflows, maintained threat model), and most findings are small documentation or test-coverage gaps. The milestone-blocking items are the snowflake determinism defect and a handful of HIGH spec/perception gaps. The one CRITICAL is SPEC-F1: snowflake detection consults wall-clock time (`_now_ms()`), so a boundary decimal renders differently on different dates — a confirmed violation of the §115 determinism MUST that the frozen golden corpus cannot catch; its spec-side half (SPEC-F6, no pinned epoch/bounds/predicate) must be fixed together. The other recommend-fix HIGHs cluster around two themes: spec-vs-implementation conformance gaps (SPEC-F2 blank-map positions emitted as booleans and recovered only via geometry; SPEC-F3 base32 disproof path lowercases where the spec mandates upper case) and CVD/perception honesty gaps (PSY-F1 blank-map max/min semantics lost under achromatopsia; PSY-F2 palette caveat omits two sub-floor CVD pairs), plus two infra HIGHs (TST-F1 Tier B golden-raster comparison is skipped in CI because cairosvg sits in an optional group; MNT-F1 fingerprint docstring omits the text-not-bytes hashing intent). The single most urgent action is to make snowflake detection deterministic — replace the wall-clock gate with a fixed upper bound and pin the epoch/bounds/predicate in the spec (SPEC-F1 + SPEC-F6) — then re-freeze the golden corpus and wire Tier B into CI so the same class of drift can't recur silently.
